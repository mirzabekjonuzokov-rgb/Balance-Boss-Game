Lesson 4: Editing for Band 9 Precision ??

?? Lesson Overview & Core Structure

Topic Focus: Technology. We use this lesson to refine arguments related to AI and digital life.

Objective: Systematically proofread for accuracy. Focus on subject-verb agreement, article usage (a/an/the), prepositions, and eliminating informal language (e.g., lots of, wanna).

The 3-Step Editing Process

  1. Read for Flow: Check Cohesion/Coherence. Do your ideas connect logically
  2. Read for Grammar: Check every verb and article. Look for missed complex structures.
  3. Read for Vocabulary: Check spelling and choose one or two simple words to upgrade with academic synonyms.

?? Grammar Focus: Noun and Relative Clauses

These clauses create sophisticated, grammatically complex sentences by embedding one idea inside another, essential for a Band 9 structure.

  • Noun Clauses: Act as the subject or object of the main clause (e.g., "What citizens demand is better public services.")
  • Relative Clauses: Provide extra information about a noun (e.g., "Governments, which are responsible for public safety, must enforce laws.").

Example of Noun Clause (Band 7+)

What is critical for technological progress is not only the development of AI but also the enhancement of data security, which raises privacy concerns.

?? Key Vocabulary & Linking Phrases

Focus on **Academic Tone** and **Strong Reporting Verbs**.

  • Academic Synonyms: important > crucial/imperative, good > beneficial/advantageous, think > contend/assert.
  • Reporting Verbs: asserts, contends, stipulates, suggests, highlights.
  • Avoiding Informal: Remove "I think," "in my opinion" (in the body paragraphs), "a lot of," "very."
