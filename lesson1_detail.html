Lesson 1: Understanding Task 2 + Writing Power Intros ??

?? Lesson Overview & Core Structure

Topic Focus: Essay Fundamentals (Band Descriptors, Question Analysis). Paraphrasing is key to Task Achievement.

Objective: Master the three-part introduction: Paraphrase, Thesis Statement, Outline Sentence.

Introduction Formula

  1. Paraphrase: Rewrite the prompt using synonyms and grammatical changes.
  2. Thesis Statement: Clearly state your main position (your answer to the question).
  3. Outline/Plan: Briefly state what your main body paragraphs will cover.

?? Grammar Focus: Sentence Variety

To score highly on Grammatical Range and Accuracy, you must use a variety of sentence structures. We focus on differentiating and combining Simple, Compound, and Complex sentences.

  • Simple: (Subject + Verb)
  • Compound: (Independent Clause + FANBOYS/semicolon + Independent Clause)
  • Complex: (Independent Clause + Subordinating Conjunction + Dependent Clause)

Example of Complex Sentence (Band 7+)

Although many people believe that technology harms children's social skills, it is undeniable that digital devices offer unprecedented access to global education.

?? Key Vocabulary & Linking Phrases

Focus on **Paraphrasing Tools** and **Basic Academic Connectors**.

  • Paraphrasing: It is widely acknowledged that, contemporary society, advocates argue that, a prevalent issue.
  • Thesis Phrases: This essay will argue that, In my opinion, the merits of X outweigh the demerits, I completely disagree with this statement.
  • Linking: To begin with, In conclusion, Furthermore, However, Ultimately.
