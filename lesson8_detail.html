Lesson 8: Advantages–Disadvantages Essays ??

?? Lesson Overview & Core Structure

Topic Focus: Crime & Punishment. We discuss the pros and cons of certain legal reforms or penalties.

Objective: Master the comparison structure: dedicate one body paragraph to advantages and one to disadvantages. Also learn the structure for the "outweigh" question (which requires stating which side is stronger).

A-D Essay Structure (Outweighing)

  1. Intro: Paraphrase + Thesis (State that the pros/cons outweigh the cons/pros).
  2. Body 1: Discuss the side with fewer points (e.g., the disadvantages).
  3. Body 2: Discuss the side you believe is stronger (e.g., the advantages).
  4. Conclusion: Summarize and reiterate your stance that one side is more significant.

?? Grammar Focus: Nominalization

Nominalization is the process of turning a verb or adjective into a noun. This is key to achieving a Band 8/9 tone as it makes your writing highly formal, abstract, and concise.

  • Verb to Noun: People decide > The people's decision
  • Adjective to Noun: The economy is stable > The stability of the economy

Example of Nominalization (Band 7+)

The implementation of stricter laws is the key to the reduction of juvenile delinquency.
(Original: When governments implement stricter laws, they can reduce juvenile delinquency.)

?? Key Vocabulary & Linking Phrases

Focus on **Advantage/Disadvantage Expressions** and **Weighing Language**.

  • Pros: A key benefit, The main advantage, An undeniable merit, The positives include.
  • Cons: A major drawback, The primary disadvantage, A serious detriment, The negatives entail.
  • Weighing: outweighs, is superseded by, is less significant than, holds greater importance.
