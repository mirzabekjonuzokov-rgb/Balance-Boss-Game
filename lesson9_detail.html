Lesson 9: Double Question Essays ?

?? Lesson Overview & Core Structure

Topic Focus: Culture & Society. We practice answering two separate, distinct questions about social trends.

Objective: Master addressing two parts of the question equally. Body 1 must answer Question 1, and Body 2 must answer Question 2. Failure to do so results in a low Task Achievement score.

Double Question Essay Structure

  1. Intro: Paraphrase + Thesis (State the answer to both Q1 and Q2).
  2. Body 1: Answer Question 1 fully (PEEL).
  3. Body 2: Answer Question 2 fully (PEEL).
  4. Conclusion: Summarize the answers to both Q1 and Q2.

?? Grammar Focus: Mixed Complex Clauses + Cohesion

This lesson focuses on combining various complex structures (conditionals, participles, noun clauses) and ensuring smooth transitions between paragraphs that deal with two separate ideas.

  • Cohesion Tool: Use clear signposting (Regarding the first question, Turning to the second point).
  • Emphasis: Employ cleft sentences (It is X that Y) for emphasis.

Example of Cleft Sentence for Emphasis (Band 7+)

It is the rapid influence of global media that causes the breakdown of traditional values in developing countries.

?? Key Vocabulary & Linking Phrases

Focus on **Evaluation Language** and **Structural Signposting**.

  • Signposting Q1: Concerning the first query, With respect to the first aspect, The primary cause is.
  • Signposting Q2: Regarding the second point, The most effective way to solve this is, Turning now to the best remedy.
  • Evaluation: crucial, paramount, essential, detrimental, negligible.
