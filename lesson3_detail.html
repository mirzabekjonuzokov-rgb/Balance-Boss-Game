Lesson 3: Writing Polished Conclusions & Improving Flow ??

?? Lesson Overview & Core Structure

Topic Focus: Cohesion (linking words) and Coherence (logical flow). We focus on the health topic for practice.

Objective: Master the two-part conclusion: Summary of main arguments and Final (often conditional) thought. Ensure smooth flow between all paragraphs.

Conclusion Formula

  1. Concluding Phrase: To conclude, In summary, Overall.
  2. Summary: Briefly reiterate the main ideas from Body 1 and Body 2 (do not introduce new ideas).
  3. Final Comment: A forward-looking statement or recommendation (optional, but highly recommended).

?? Grammar Focus: Participles and Reduced Clauses

Participle clauses (starting with -ing or -ed) reduce the length and improve the sophistication of sentences, moving beyond simple compound sentences.

  • -ing clause: Used to show cause, effect, or simultaneous action.
  • -ed clause: Used to show cause or to modify a noun (reduced relative clause).

Example of Participle Clause (Band 7+)

Suffering from a lack of physical activity, the public is now facing a rise in chronic diseases.
(Original: Because the public suffers from a lack of physical activity,...)

?? Key Vocabulary & Linking Phrases

Focus on **Concluding Phrases** and **Advanced Cohesive Devices**.

  • Concluding: To conclude, In summary, Overall, To sum up, Therefore, In light of the above.
  • Contrasting Cohesion: Despite this, In contrast, Nonetheless, On the flip side.
  • Adding Ideas: Moreover, Furthermore, In addition to this, As well as.
