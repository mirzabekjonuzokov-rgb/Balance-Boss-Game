Lesson 6: Discussion Essays (Both Views + Opinion) ??

?? Lesson Overview & Core Structure

Topic Focus: Globalization. We discuss the impact of globalization on local cultures and economies.

Objective: Master the balanced discussion structure: dedicate one body paragraph to each view and clearly state your personal opinion in the introduction and conclusion.

Discussion Essay Structure

  1. Intro: Paraphrase + Thesis (Mention both views, state your opinion clearly).
  2. Body 1: Discuss the first view (e.g., arguments for globalization).
  3. Body 2: Discuss the second view (e.g., arguments against globalization).
  4. Conclusion: Summarize both sides and reaffirm your personal opinion (must match the thesis).

?? Grammar Focus: Adverb and Relative Clauses (Advanced)

Adverbial clauses provide background information (time, manner, condition) and relative clauses define or describe nouns, making sentences dense with information and highly academic.

  • Adverbial Clause: Often start with *as, since, after, before, although, when*.
  • Relative Clause: Often start with *who, which, that, whose*.

Example of Adverbial and Relative Clause (Band 7+)

Although many nations benefit from globalization, which expands international trade, local cultures often suffer since Western media strongly influence social behavior.

?? Key Vocabulary & Linking Phrases

Focus on **Discussion Connectors** and phrases for **Balance**.

  • Introducing Sides: On the one hand, Conversely, On the other side of the argument, There are proponents who argue that.
  • Balancing: While these points are valid, it should also be noted that, Despite the strengths of this argument.
  • Opinion Reinforcement: This essay believes, From my perspective, I concur with the former/latter view.
