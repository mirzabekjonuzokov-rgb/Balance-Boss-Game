Lesson 5: Opinion Essays (Agree/Disagree Type) ??

?? Lesson Overview & Core Structure

Topic Focus: Economy & Employment. We practice stating a clear stance on economic policies.

Objective: Master the structure for opinion essays. Choose a clear position (strongly agree/disagree or partial agreement) and maintain it throughout the essay.

Opinion Essay Structure (Strong Stance)

  1. Intro: Paraphrase + Clear Thesis (I strongly agree/disagree).
  2. Body 1: Main reason supporting your view (PEEL).
  3. Body 2: Second reason supporting your view (PEEL).
  4. Body 3 (Optional): A concession paragraph to show balance, followed by a rebuttal to reinforce your stance.
  5. Conclusion: Summary of your arguments and restatement of your opinion.

?? Grammar Focus: Contrast Connectors

These connectors (such as *whereas*, *while*, *despite/in spite of*) are essential for introducing a concession or contrasting ideas, a key feature of balanced academic writing.

  • Clause Connectors: While/Whereas (used to link two full clauses).
  • Phrase Connectors: Despite/In spite of (followed by a noun or -ing verb).

Example of Contrast Connector (Band 7+)

Despite efforts to control inflation rates, economic growth has remained stagnant in many regions, whereas job opportunities have expanded only in the service sector.

?? Key Vocabulary & Linking Phrases

Focus on **Argument Language** to clearly express your position.

  • Stating Opinion: I firmly believe, It is my contention that, I strongly assert, The primary viewpoint of this essay is that.
  • Concession: It may be argued that, While there is some truth to the fact that, Opponents often contend that.
  • Reinforcing: Nevertheless, However, Nonetheless, This does not negate the fact that.
