Lesson 7: Problem–Solution Essays ??

?? Lesson Overview & Core Structure

Topic Focus: Government & Society. We analyze social issues like inequality and crime.

Objective: Master the logical link between identifying a problem and proposing a realistic, targeted solution. Ensure the solution directly addresses the stated problem/cause.

Problem-Solution Essay Structure

  1. Intro: Paraphrase + Thesis (The essay will outline the main problems and suggest effective remedies).
  2. Body 1: Problem Analysis (Identify the main problem and its core causes).
  3. Body 2: Solution Proposal (Propose solutions that directly tackle the causes identified in Body 1).
  4. Conclusion: Summary of problem and solution.

?? Grammar Focus: Passive Voice + Participle Phrases

The passive voice is crucial in problem/solution essays because the focus should be on the action (the solution or the problem) rather than the actor (who does it), maintaining a formal, academic tone.

  • Passive: The problem should be addressed by the government. (Focus on the problem/solution)
  • Active with Participle: The government, addressing the problem, must enforce the rule of law. (Adding sophistication)

Example of Passive Voice and Participle (Band 7+)

The high crime rates, driven by social inequality, must be addressed by implementing tougher penalties, resulting in improved public safety.

?? Key Vocabulary & Linking Phrases

Focus on **Solution Verbs** and **Consequence Language**.

  • Problem Verbs: encounter, grapple with, face, confront, driven by.
  • Solution Verbs: address, implement, mitigate, tackle, curb, alleviate.
  • Consequences: leading to, thus reducing, which would ensure, in an effort to.
