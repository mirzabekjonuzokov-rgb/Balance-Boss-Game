Lesson 2: Developing Concrete Body Paragraphs ???

?? Lesson Overview & Core Structure

Topic Focus: Idea Generation and Logical Flow (Cohesion and Coherence). We focus on the environment topic for practice.

Objective: Master the PEEL structure: Point, Explanation, Example, Link. Ensure ideas are fully developed and directly support the thesis.

Body Paragraph Formula (PEEL)

  1. P - Point: The topic sentence (main idea of the paragraph).
  2. E - Explanation: Explain the point in detail (the 'how' or 'why').
  3. E - Example: Provide specific, relevant support (e.g., studies, statistics, specific cases).
  4. L - Link: Conclude and link back to the main thesis.

?? Grammar Focus: Complex Clauses (Conditionals)

Conditional sentences (using if, unless, provided that, etc.) are essential for discussing causes, effects, and hypothetical solutions in IELTS essays.

  • First Conditional (Real/Possible): If people recycle more, waste production will be minimized.
  • Second Conditional (Unreal/Hypothetical): If governments addressed environmental issues, the planet would recover faster.

Example of Conditional Clause (Band 7+)

Unless renewable energy sources are adopted globally, the world's dependence on fossil fuels will continue to negatively influence climate change.

?? Key Vocabulary & Linking Phrases

Focus on **Cause and Effect** vocabulary, crucial for logical arguments.

  • Cause/Reason: due to, owing to, as a result of, on account of.
  • Effect/Consequence: leads to, gives rise to, consequently, the consequence is that.
  • Emphasis: notably, particularly, primarily, significantly.
